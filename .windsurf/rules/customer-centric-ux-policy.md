7) Customer-centric UX is a hard requirement.
- For customer-facing flows, optimize for clarity, trust, and low friction:
  - friendly copy (no internal/dev wording),
  - sensible defaults,
  - clear primary CTA + secondary actions,
  - explicit loading, success, and error states.
- Avoid exposing internal IDs/URLs/“localhost”-style artifacts in UI.
 
8) UX decision checkpoint for complex work.
- For complex tasks, I must state the “user journey” in 3-6 bullets before coding:
  - entry point,
  - happy path,
  - failure states,
  - recovery path,
  - what the user sees at each step.
